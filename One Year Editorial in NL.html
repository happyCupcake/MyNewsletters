Covid Updates

staff writer Prisha sanghvi

Covid-19 has wreaked havoc on the world. Businesses have shut down, people have been separated from their loved ones, and the economy has been brought to its knees. The government is taking every measure they can to help the nation and its people recover from this dark time.


President Biden is working on distributing vaccines at an accelerated rate to further the chances of reopening everything. But due to the vaccine distribution happening daily in different parts of the country, people are starting to meet up in large groups, sometimes without masks.


Many schools and colleges are opening up the hybrid curriculum, but with extreme caution and minimal student entry. However, some parents prefer to keep their kids at home for school in terms of safety for themselves and their family. According to edsource.org, people are also reluctant to send their kids if they are having financial difficulties due to loss of job in the family.


Many restaurants are opening, but mainly for outdoor seating and take-out. However, it is a cause-effect situation. Restaurants open their dining, which prompts a surge in covid cases, which then forces them to shut down again. Then the cases will go down, which will encourage them to open outdoor dining again, which will restart the vicous cycle. However, outdoor dining is essential for small businesses to stay afloat in this economic wave-pool.


Another surge in the country is in traveling. According to the New York Times, there have been dramatic increases in flight bookings due to the vaccinations. However, the government is advising against encouraging flight bookings until the number of cases are under control and are not rising daily.





Page 2

This pandemic is taking a toll on the mental health of many people in the nation. People are being separated from their loved ones and have not been able to meet them in months. This is aggravating mental anxiety and clinical depression. People are becoming more socially awkward and impatient with others as a result of the social distancing protocol. This is bringing a increase in people searching for a companion….an animal companion. From those who live alone to big families, everyone is apparently getting some sort of pet, the most common being a cat or a dog.


In conclusion, masks are still encouraged even if you and/or the other person are vaccinated. The government still discourages large gatherings of people and to wash hands and sanitize thoroughly and properly when leaving/returning home.

BY HOLLY BLACK

STAFF WRITER PRISHA SANGHVI


The Cruel Prince by Holly Black is an exceptional read. It features a girl by the name of Jude and a faerie named Cardan. At first, there is instant hate. Then this hate develops into attraction. However, this development takes a lot. It is a very intriguing read filled with adventure, romance, and tragedy.

The main character himself does a few bad things. The villains, who were detested initially, start to appear more human as Darrow interacts with them. Even when the book dives into action, it is impossible to distinguish between the good and the bad. Darrow is far from perfect and is one of the most convincing main characters. Many of his decisions are propelled by anger, but beyond that, by loyalty, love, and betrayal. His selflessness to help everyone wasn’t there at the beginning of the story, but it appears when he is reminded of what he has lost. Throughout the story, he goes on to make many flawed decisions. These are the properties of a brilliant character design. The book isn’t perfect, so it has its flaws. A major one was the blatant sexism shining through the story. It may have been historically or futuristically accurate, but that was not clear from the way it was written. Many of the female characters were simply used as love interests for the other male characters. Another flaw is, the book moves forward quite slowly. The part that attracts the most people to the book doesn’t come in until 30% into the book. This means that 30% of the book is used as setup. Besides these things, it is a great read and is very obviously influenced by many famous series including, Percy Jackson, the Hunger Games, Harry Potter, and even Star Wars! Many of this may have to do with the fact that some of the series mentioned above also drew influences from each other. Everything in the story is based on Greek mythology and Roman interpretations. The characters even compare the two versions of the gods two each other. The characters are also placed in groups who compete for a prize throughout the book. Many aspects of space sci- fi have clear influences from Star Wars, although this becomes more apparent in future books. The fighting and gore also seem to follow a similar path to the Hunger Games. Despite the similarities, the gore is amplified tenfold in Red Rising. Finally, Red Rising contains many allusions to historical events and ancient battle strategies used to tie all its themes together. All in all, Red Rising marks the charts as one of the best dystopian books to be written.

Red rising by pierce brown

staff writer mrinali jain

Red Rising is a fantastic book. It takes the classic Dystopian theme of a hierarchy and spins it into a gripping story about a boy from the worker class who learns of the terrible injustice to him and his family and friends. Darrow, a headstrong young man from the Red class, works along with his friends, hoping to make Mars a more suitable place for people to live. As far as he knows, his kind has been doing this for centuries. And he’s right, but it shouldn’t be this way. On the surface, civilization has already sprung up in full force, and the Reds are being used for free labor. Enraged and heartbroken from the losses of loved ones, Darrow is determined to do anything to justify their deaths, even if this means infiltrating the top, disguised as Gold. The author, Pierce Brown, has a unique writing style. Many of his sentences are short and end suddenly, which gives each statement a very powerful connotation. “I would have lived in peace. But my enemies brought me war.” (Brown 1). The setting, which is one of the first things that appear in the book, is described with such vibrancy! Pierce Brown takes “show don’t tell” to the next level when he describes the hot and dusty underground of Mars compared to the vast and glittering cities on the surface. The setting will get any sci-fi fan entranced by the contrasting, almost post-apocalyptic, image the book projects. The setting and the writing style are great, but Brown’s fundamental skills shine through his character design. Each character is given personalities instead of single, defining traits. Although some characters of characters only appear in the book for a short period, the reader finds themselves attached to all of them.

One year

staff writer mrinali jain

One year ago. Has it been one year! On March 13, 2020, I didn’t go to school. We all thought it would be a simple, two-week lockdown to flatten the curve for COVID -19 has now officially lasted one year long. A lot has happened in the past year—more than we could have ever imagined. From environmental problems such as fires to our new President's election, we have dealt with a lot. Let’s rewind and take a look at everything that has happened to us thus far.


On March 13, 2020, the President declared the COVID -19 outbreak a national emergency. This gave him access to additional funds that could be used to deal with the situation. At that time, in Alameda, there were less than 20 positive cases. Not long after, Gov. Gavin Newsom announced a state-wide lockdown and took some preemptive steps to stop the spread. For most people who did not go to school, this was the beginning of the lockdown. Around March 25, the government started handing out stimulus checks to help small businesses and people with low-income. On the 26th, the number of global cases passed 500,000. By this point, many of us were getting restless, and the impact of the situation was hitting us. Luckily, all hope was not lost. When we couldn’t meet our friends and family in person, many of us turned to the internet for support. Many major online platforms helped us keep the communication going with our friends and helped us enjoy our lockdown. This included watching movies with our friends, finding easier ways to video call, and watching our favorite streamers and Youtubers together. Many how-to videos were coming out at this point, too! For those struggling with a lack of haircuts, the number of hair tutorials skyrocketed. Not to mention all the home-care blogs and videos that popped up.



Page 3

Our faith in humanity increased ten-fold when tough times were faced with many good samaritans willing to sacrifice their time, energy, and money, for the better of the world. Many volunteers sewed masks and donated money to help hospitals and schools. As a result, many people were able to learn new skills such as sewing, and they were able to help their community. By April 2, the number of global cases had surpassed 1,000,000! On May 25, George Floyd was murdered by a police officer. This event blew the nation, and everyone mourned the loss of a fellow human being. The murder of an innocent sparked outrage, and the protests began. By May 30, looters under the guise of being protesters caused many cities to impose a curfew for their citizens' safety. As the BLM movement gained traction, a part of Washington D. C. was designated as the Black Lives Matter Plaza. On July 1, California Governor Gavin Newsom doubled down on lockdown restrictions. By August 3, many Californians were grappling for unemployment benefits. On August 11, Joe Biden announced Californian senator Kamala Harris as his vice-presidential running mate. On August 16, a series of wildfires plagued California. The disastrous August Complex fire follows this. On September 18, Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg died at 87. By then, the campaigns for both presidential candidates were coming in strong. In early October, our then President was tested positive for COVID 19. November arrives. The long-awaited election day. By the end of the day, Biden was in the lead, but a clear winner had not been determined. Millions of mail-in ballots had yet to be counted. Trump disagrees with the results and claims that there may have been voter fraud with mail-in ballots. By November 7, it was confirmed that President Biden had won the election. On January 20, he was officially inaugurated and began taking action on his vaccination plan. Although this past year has had its ups and downs, we all got through it. Now the only way to go is up!
